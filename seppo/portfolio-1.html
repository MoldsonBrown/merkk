Buenos Aires & La Pampa


A pulsating city of mystery and romance with La Pampa’s rural charm on the side

For many, Buenos Aires is the city of tango, style and an incredible avant-garde cuisine and artistic scene. But it’s also the name of the adjacent province, Provincia de Buenos Aires, known for La Pampa region with its endless swathes of lush farmland and rural villages rooted deeply in Argentina’s early history including some of the country’s finest estancias.
Disembark in Buenos Aires from your long-haul flight to rest in trendy Palermo or San Telmo, or the ritzy Recoleta. Stroll the streets of Latin America’s most cosmopolitan capital.
Sense in its buildings and trends a people’s longing for their distant European past, the city’s growing embrace of its Latin American identity, and the mélange of cultures, sounds and cuisines: from the colonial Spanish, to the migrant Italian, Armenian, Jews, Lebanese, Russians, Bolivians.

Discover cutting-edge experimental gastronomy or intimate closed-door restaurants. Or the city’s underground music scene and award-winning independent theatre. And then take it down a notch or two — La Pampa! Journey out into La Pampa region of Buenos Aires to taste the traditions and practices that built Argentina.
Stay at a family estancia dating back to colonial times, with grand European homesteads located in the heart of lush flat grasslands that run as far as the eye can see. Nestle by the campfire for an asado (barbeque) after a day of horse riding with Argentina’s famous gauchos. While you’re here, visit villages, goldsmiths, and arts & crafts centers.

CRAFTED IDEAS FOR YOU

In Buenos Aires:

▸ Take tango classes or get swept away by a local legend at one of the city’s famous milongas

▸ Immerse yourself in local history with Buenos Aires architecture tours including the Colón Theater, Palacio Bartolo and Casa Rosada

▸ Get some happy snaps in front of the street art in neighborhoods like Palermo, Almagro, Villa Crespo and Chacarita

▸ Find the tombs of Eva Perón and other historical figures in one of the world’s most beautiful cemeteries, Recoleta

▸ Or just be a local. Visit the city’s beautiful bookstores, grab a cheesy slice of pizza along Corrientes Avenue, enjoy the unique vibe of Buenos Aires’s cafés notorious and bodegones, or watch a football match at La Bombonera

In La Pampa:

▸ Drop the BA pace by visiting quaint towns and cities like Tandil, Epecuén, Capilla del Señor and San Antonio de Areco

▸ Take your horse riding skills to new heights with polo lessons

▸ For meat lovers, learn how to prepare the gaucho-style a la cruz barbecue

▸ Spend the night at a traditional colonial-style estancia
