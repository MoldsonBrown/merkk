Patagonia's Lake District


Pristine lakes, ancient forests and unassumingly stylish locals

Patagonia’s Lake District is beauty every which way you look. Nestled in the north-western region of Patagonia, the Lakes District covers a compact but diverse section of southern Chile and Argentina. The region stands out for its high concentration of native forests, volcanoes, hundreds of mountain trails and a string of quaint villages located in the most breathtaking mountain-side scenery. Exquisite fresh food, craft beer production, colossal alpine hotels — the region’s communities and habits are unassumingly stylish, and instead let the geography do all the talking.

Craft & Travel’s starting points in Patagonia’s Lake District are the small cities and villages that straddle the region’s famous lakes. For example, Bariloche, a hub for skiers and adventurers, and a perfect point of entry or departure in the region.

From the picturesque forest village of Villa La Angostura, travel north along the majestic Route of the Seven Lakes, through the dreamy Andes mountains to Chile’s volcano town Pucón and southward to the rich Valdivian forests (Reserva Costera Valdiviana) and Lake Llanquihue.

From there, we offer a number of journeys in the area of Lake Llanquihue, crossing to Chiloé and Mocha Islands — the starting point for cruises through the fjords.

Editor’s note: There’s no one way to experience Patagonia’s Lakes District but we certainly recommend self-drive for greater autonomy and the chance to take it all in on your own terms.
