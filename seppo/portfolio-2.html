Córdoba & Cuyo


A crossroad of stories and sensations at the heart of Argentina

Situated in its geographic center, Córdoba province is Argentina’s heartland in more ways than one. With its deep colonial and religious history, the region serves up countless UNESCO World Heritage sites tied to the former Jesuit missions, plus the majesty of some of the most lush and charming geography in the continent, the Sierras.
The neighboring wine-producing Cuyo region is also on our radar. With large swathes of Cuyo unspoilt by mass tourism, we’re keeping it and its surprises just for you.

Córdoba province, with Córdoba City as its capital, is your access point to the Sierras and Cuyo regions, and easily connects you to Buenos Aires or the Mendoza wine region and on to Santiago de Chile. The region, ideal for short or transit stays, has largely remained off the radar of visitors to the country despite its geographical and cultural offerings, including the remnants of the Jesuit Missions and destinations like Ongamira with its draw-dropping rock formations.

Perhaps unlike any other region in Argentina, Córdoba’s traditional estancias and lodges are a must for visitors seeking exclusivity and relaxation, as well as specific activities like yoga, painting classes, trekking and horseback and donkey riding.

When it comes to Cuyo, Sierra de las Quijadas National Park in San Luis is one of our top picks! And you cannot go past the Talampaya National Park and the Ischigualasto Provincial Park, otherwise known as the Valley of the Moon (Valle de la luna) - both declared UNESCO World Heritage Sites in 2000. Cuyo is indeed all about the geography and is especially interesting for lovers of paleontology, geology and anthropology.
