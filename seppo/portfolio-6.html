Central Patagonia, Ruta 40 & Carretera Austral


The solitude and mystery of Route 40 and a labyrinth of natural wonders along the Carretera Austral

The Patagonian wilderness at its most dramatic and undiscovered. That’s Central Patagonia — connected in Argentina by the mythical Route 40 through the vast expanses of the Patagonian steppe and manifest on the Chilean side in a complex labyrinth of vivid lush forests and remote islands connected, in parts, by the Carretera Austral highway. Untouched solitude. Unforgettable encounters with the people and the places of a truly awe-inspiring territory.

Craft & Travel’s journeys through this mystical region of the Southern Hemisphere showcase how united Argentina and Chile are in a shared experience of isolation and natural beauty.

Much of the region’s character is concentrated in its multiple national parks, including one of the continent’s newest and largest: the bi-national Patagonia Park, a lonely trekker’s paradise. You can also discover old classics like Los Alerces, a refuge for some of the region’s most precious flora and fauna, and the nearby mountain town of Esquel. For fjords, canals and pristine forests, there’s the Queulat National Park. And by the Pacific Ocean, explore Corcovado National Park near Chaitén for some of nature’s best volcanic charm.

Editor’s note: There’s no one way to experience Central Patagonia but we certainly recommend self-drive for greater autonomy and the chance to take it all in on your own terms.
