Northern Argentina & Chile


Beauty and tradition at every turn

Mountains, forests, deserts, salt flats and plateaus make up northern Argentina and Chile, a region home to some of the continent’s oldest, most complex and culturally rich societies. With the remnants of the Inca Road System running through these dramatic landscapes, the region consists of Argentina’s Tucumán, Salta and Jujuy provinces and La Puna de Atacama plateau in Chile. Mountain villages suspended in time, unique traditions in arts and crafts, and adventure sports - the north has it all. And finally, let us introduce Catamarca province, an undiscovered desert treasure!
From Salta capital northbound, experience the Salinas Grandes salt flats and La Puna desert plateaus. Discover the quaint village born from the former Salta-Antofagasta Railway, once the most important infrastructure project in the region connecting Argentina and Chile. Heading south, find the breathtaking rock formations of the Calchaquíes Valleys, home to the famous Torrontés wine variety.

These majestic valleys extend into Tucumán province’s Tafí del Valle region, renowned for its wet forests and wildlife. Further south is Catamarca province, arguably Argentina’s best kept secret region serving up some of the world’s most monumental desert landscapes.
Backtracking slightly to the Chilean side of the Andes, we have the unmissable San Pedro de Atacama, the world’s driest desert and a gateway to high-plateau adventures including trekking plus astronomy.

Editor’s note: There’s no one way to experience Northern Argentina and Chile but we certainly recommend self-drive for greater autonomy and the chance to take it all in on your own terms.


CRAFTED IDEAS FOR YOU

In Argentina:

▸ Visit villages in the Quebrada de Humahuaca and Calchaquíes Valleys including multi-day horse riding adventures

▸ Los Cardones National Park

▸ Visit the communities surrounding the famous “Train to the Clouds”, a remnant of one of the region’s most remarkable engineering projects

▸ Take classes in the region’s unique and diverse arts and crafts traditions including ceramics, weaving and knits

▸ Visit the main attractions of Tolar Grande at the footsteps of Mt. Lullaillaco including 4x4 adventures

▸ Campo de Piedra Pomez in Catamarca province

In Chile:

▸ San Pedro de Atacama: experience the world’s driest desert including the Moon and Death valleys, Tatio Gayser and Licancabur volcano

▸ Take a picnic at Rainbow Valley

▸ Astronomy tours on the outskirts of Atacama

▸ Flamenco spotting at the region’s high-plateau lagoons including Chaxa, Miscanti and Miñiques

▸ Entry point to the world’s largest salt flats: Uyuni in Bolivia
