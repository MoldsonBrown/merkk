Iguazú Falls & Iberá Wetlands


A captivating indigeous heartland with nature at its most exuberant

Rich red dirt roads meet lush sub-tropical jungles through the exuberant Iberá wetlands all the way to the mighty Iguazú Falls. While Iguazú rightfully gets everyone’s attention, there’s so much more to this natural paradise in far north-eastern Argentina. Delving deep into history, beyond more recent waves of Polish and German inmigration, two major traditions combine in the most unique ways: Spanish colonialism and the region’s indigenous Guaraní culture. Today, Iguazú and surroundings are about so much more than the falls!
Iguazú is undeniably one of Argentina’s most alluring natural attractions — and frankly, there’s no wonder.

But once you’ve seen and experienced the breathtaking falls on either or both the Argentine and Brazilian sides, this region offers you so much more.
The Iberá Wetlands, the second largest marshland in the world, draws dozens of film crews each year for its pristine and unique landscapes. In and around Iguazú, you’ll also come into contact with the Guaraní culture — which spans Argentina, Paraguay and Brazil — in the form of emergent cuisine types plus arts and crafts. The region’s colonial past is present at the Jesuit ruins and, if you’re up for it, you can also visit Che Guevara’s former jungle home.


CRAFTED IDEAS FOR YOU

▸ Get drenched in the Devil’s Throat at Iguazú Falls

▸ Take in all the angles visiting both the Argentine and Brazilian sides of the falls

▸ Visit colonial-era estancias like Santa Cecilia in Posadas or San Juan Poriahu in Ituzaingó

▸ Wander through rock-red Jesuit ruins and ponder the region’s unique history

▸ Discover Guaraní cuisine — taste surubí, boga, mate, yuca and yacaratia

▸ Bike through the lush Yriapu forest

▸ Learn about the production of yerba maté, the popular drink consumed across the Southern Cone countries
