Southern Patagonia & Antarctica


Blissful solitude and nature at its most monumental

Southern Patagonia is as pristine a region as they come. From the village of El Chaltén through to the world’s southernmost city Ushuaia, the far south is home to landscapes of the most dramatic: glaciers, steppes, fjords.

And the famous granite peaks of Torres del Paine and Fitz Roy. Nature is our definition of luxury and Southern Patagonia is as high-end as it comes.
It’s impossible to pin-point one single standout attraction in this monumental region of Patagonia.

For one, there’s the glaciers of the Southern Patagonia Ice Field, each with its own characteristics and all completely unmissable.

Or the granite peaks of Torres del Paine and Fitz Roy. The region is best experienced from the village of El Chaltén heading south or from the unique port city Ushuaia heading north and zigzagging from one breathtaking site to another through estancias, villages and the pure air of Patagonian.


CRAFTED IDEAS FOR YOU

▸ Ice trekking in Perito Moreno or Grey Glacier & Kayaking between glaciers on the region’s turquoise lakes and rivers

▸ Overnight in a traditional Patagonia estancia / Horseback riding from estancia to estancia

▸ El Chaltén to Villa O’Higgins: Trekking or cycling through the land of ice and water

▸ Hiking “the W” circuit in Torres del Paine National Park for amazing views of Las Torres peaks, the Horns of Paine and the French Valley

▸ Hiking the Dientes de Navarino

▸ Enjoy the flavors of Centolla and delicious fresh food in the Puerto Almanza port near Ushuaia

▸ Cross the Beagle Channel to Bahia Ensenada in the world’s southernmost national park in Tierra del Fuego

▸ Visit penguin colonies and explore nearby fjords

▸ Try your luck reaching Cabo de Hornos by cruise
