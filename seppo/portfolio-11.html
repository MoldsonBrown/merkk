Uruguay


Simplicity meets authenticity just over the river from Argentina

A tiny patch of lush green grass nestled between the two giants of Argentina and Brazil, the unassumingly enchanting Uruguay combines natural beauty, rich traditions and incredibly well preserved colonial history in places like the UNESCO World Heritage-listed village of Colonia, just an hour’s ferry ride from Buenos Aires. Where yerba mate is even more popular than in Argentina and life is just so much more laid-back — that’s Uruguay! Forget the high-end, high-brow beaches of Punta del Este — Uruguay’s true beauty emerges along its rugged northern coastline at places like La Pedrera and nearby eco-communities living off the land and off small-scale sustainable tourism.

Or deep in its rural heartland on the country’s beautiful estates and estancias where the traditions you’ll experience are as authentic as they come. Colonia, whether as a day trip from Buenos Aires or part of an exclusive Uruguayan itinerary, is a must for its pristine historic center, cobble-stone streets and photo-worthy colored facades. Uruguay is also home to exceptionally fine wine and fresh produce, and is extremely family and LGBT friendly.

Editor’s note: There’s no one way to experience Uruguay but we certainly recommend self-drive for greater autonomy and the chance to take it all in on your own terms.


CRAFTED IDEAS FOR YOU

▸ Relax deeply at the large and isolated La Pedrera, José Ignacio and northern coastal towns

▸ Take in rural life in luxury village stays near José Ignacio and Laguna Garzón

▸ Experience dynamic rural traditions from colonial times and high-end multi-day stays at Uruguay’s estancias

▸ Enjoy the quiet and charming, laid-back capital Montevideo - or consider the city in February for the country’s vibrant carnaval with candombe, murga and tablados

▸ Visit Colonia by ferry from Buenos Aires or a short drive or bus from Montevideo

▸ Discover Guaraní cuisine — taste surubí, boga, mate, yuca and yacaratia

▸ Bike through the lush Yriapu forest

▸ Learn about the production of yerba maté, the popular drink consumed across the Southern Cone countries
